\begin{abstract}
Let $H=\ell^2(\Z)$ and let $S$ be the bilateral shift.  We prove that the unit-sphere quotient
\[
        \Sphere(H)/\langle S\rangle
\]
with the chordal quotient metric has infinite Hilbert distortion.  Equivalently,
its finite subsets have unbounded Euclidean distortion.  The same conclusion
holds for the intrinsic spherical quotient metric, since the chordal and angular
metrics on a sphere are universally bilipschitz equivalent, and this equivalence
passes to quotients.  The proof is short: finite pieces of hard flat tori are
realized, after rescaling and arbitrarily small error, inside higher-rank
translation quotients of Hilbert spheres.  The only role of an arbitrary lattice
is to put a flat metric on the unit lattice torus; a mixed-radix coding then
embeds each finite higher-rank piece into the one-dimensional shift quotient.
\end{abstract}
